The full poem

In the twilight of her own reflections, she stands, a mosaic of the East and West in tender hands. Her eyes, a tapestry of dusk and dawn entwined, harbor depths of the most beautiful, troubled kind.

A fortress of ivory, she wears as her shield, yet beneath the ramparts, her true self is concealed. A porcelain warrior, feigning strength in her stare, but touch her too sharply, and cracks may appear.

Her soul's rivers, navigated in glass. Every pulse of emotion, a boundary to unmask. Once dreaming of flames, akin to her fire. Now embers in ashes, no twin to inspire.

A mirror turned inward, her gaze often lingers. With the brush of self-doubt, painted by her own fingers. She is the moon's reflection on a still, silent lake, slow to reveal the depths of the secrets she keeps.

An architect of thought, crafting castles in the air. She wanders the labyrinths of her mind with care. Restless nights whisper through her thoughts, unsung. In the quiet hours, her heartstrings tightly strung.

A symphony of silence, where her mind races free. Tossing on the tides of what is and what could be. A creature of beauty, lost in the maze of time. With eyes that are deep and troubled, yet utterly sublime.
